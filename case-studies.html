Safe and sustainable supply of building materials

Funded by a European Open Science Cloud (EOSC) Futures grant administered by GÃ‰ANT.

Geochron was awarded an EU tender to conduct a survey of representative quarries supplying building materials in Ireland. Surveys are designed to assist quarries to demonstrate compliance with relevant environmental and radiation protection legislation, or for planning applications for new quarries or expansion of existing quarries.The project benefits from state of the art unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) technology for customised surveys of quarries in Ireland.

Three separate high spatial resolution datasets are collected.

Two Dimensional Maps

Individual digital images integrated into a orthorectified photo mosaic for up to date true colour 2D maps. Maps can be used for environmental impact surveys.

Point Cloud Data

Laser scanner (LIDAR) point cloud data which are ideal for detailed 3D images of the survey area. Models can be used as a resource tool for resource management.

Gamma Radiation

Gamma spectrometry mapping, to produce reliable maps of gamma dose rate, potassium (K), uranium (U), and thorium (Th) concentration of the quarry surface. Maps can be used in 2D or 3D, as a resource tool, and to check compliance with relevant radiation protection legislation.
